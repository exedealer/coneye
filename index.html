How it works: Your retina has three cone types — S, M, L — each tuned to a different wavelength range. Each incoming photon fires each cone in proportion to the overlap with its sensitivity curve. The brain reads three numbers (like RGB) and constructs the perceived color. Multiple photons mix their activations — that's how white light and off-spectrum colors like pink arise.
